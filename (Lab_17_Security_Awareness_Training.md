Lab 17 — Security Awareness Training
Objective

To understand and deliver security awareness training by educating users on common threats, testing their knowledge, and identifying security gaps through feedback.

Tools & Environment
Google Slides / LibreOffice Impress
Google Forms / Survey Tool
Web Browser
Sample Users (Test Audience)
Task 1 — Create a Security Awareness Presentation
Step 1: Choose a Topic
Selected topic:
Phishing (or Password Hygiene / Social Engineering)
Step 2: Develop Presentation
Created slides covering:
Introduction to threat
How the attack works
Real-world examples
Best practices for prevention
Example Content
Slide 1: Introduction to Phishing
Slide 2: Attack flow diagram
Slide 3: Real-world phishing example
Slide 4: Prevention techniques
Included visuals (diagrams, infographics) for clarity

✅ Result: Awareness presentation created.

Task 2 — Conduct Quiz / Scenario-Based Testing
Step 1: Design Quiz
Created questions:
Multiple choice
True/False
Scenario-based
Example Scenario
“You receive an email from your bank asking for login details. What should you do?”
Step 2: Conduct Quiz
Used Google Forms to distribute quiz
Simulated user participation

✅ Result: User understanding tested through practical scenarios.

Task 3 — Collect Feedback & Identify Gaps
Step 1: Gather Feedback
Asked participants:
Was the training clear?
Do you feel more secure?
Step 2: Analyze Results
Reviewed quiz responses
Identified common mistakes and weak areas
Documented knowledge gaps for improvement

✅ Result: Training effectiveness evaluated.

Key Security Concepts Learned
Security awareness fundamentals
Phishing and social engineering attacks
Password hygiene best practices
Human factor in cybersecurity
Training effectiveness evaluation
Security Impact
Before:
Users unaware of common threats
High risk of phishing/social engineering attacks
After:
Improved user awareness
Reduced likelihood of human-based attacks
Identified gaps for future training
Conclusion

In this lab, I created and delivered a security awareness program, tested user knowledge through quizzes, and analyzed feedback to identify gaps. This strengthens the human layer of security, which is critical in preventing social engineering attacks in a SOC environment.
